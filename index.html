Bitmap

Where creativity and community connect.

Bitmap is a social media app that lets users explore works of digital art throught augmented reality in physical environment around them. With Bitmap open on their phone, users will see a feed of geo-specific posts that fellow creatives have made and posted.

My Team

I worked on research, ideation, design and testing phases on this project. The tools my team used included Figma, Miro, and Trello.

Objectives & Research

Our initial objectives in user research were to understand users views on art an creativity, augmented reality and what aspects of social media platforms they like and dislike.


75 users were surveyed and five interviews were conducted in person and over zoom. We asked our users questions to understand their basic demographics, their relationship to augmented reality, social media and what creativity looked like in their day to day lives.

User Persona

Our target users are creative individuals who are seeking to make and share works of art in a new way like our friend Liam below. Our users are people who are currently active on other social media platforms such as Instagram but are losing interest in the due to it becoming one giant ad.

Sketches & Iteration

With all of our research organized and visualized we began prototyping with sketched mock-ups. We focused on key features in the app like a “Creator View” where users would be using brushes, text, and emoji’s to bring their creative ideas to life in AR.

We knew from the beginning that the Bitmap app would be a fairly new and novel experience so our initial user flow would be an initial tutorial for users who had just downloaded the app. With that in mind our initial low-fi wireframes showed the process of getting started using the app. From making posts, viewing profiles, and finding artwork near the geographic locations our users are in.

After testing the final prototype the only questions we received were about details regarding the concept of the application. All of our tester’s feedback indicated our prototype was easy to navigate and they clearly understood the tools to view or create art in the app. Icons were described as familiar and straightforward and no one had to think about how to move around.

Conclusion

A geo-specific social media app that is based in augmented reality is an incredibly complex concept. When we landed on this idea, it seemed like there were hundreds of rabbit holes we could have gone down. After a lot of discussion and competitor research, we felt we found a niche that would serve creative users in a unique way. Through our iterative process, we decided it was best to make the interface and icons as familiar as possible so our users’ focus remained on creativity and not the learning curve of another app.

For the next steps in our project, we’d like to take some time to further build out the social aspect of the app, including a unique messaging function where users could message each other only through exchanging drawings. This app was a lot of fun to think about, design and share with users. We’re excited to take it even further.
